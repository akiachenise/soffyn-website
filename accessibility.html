Accessibility at soffyn.

Accessibility isn't an afterthought here. It's the reason soffyn exists.

Effective Date: March 1, 2026 | Last Updated: March 1, 2026 | Developed By Akia, LLC

Accessibility isn't an afterthought at soffyn — it's in our DNA.

soffyn was built by Akia, a neurodivergent Black woman, developer, and accessibility consultant. Every design decision, every interaction, every line of copy is made with the full range of human brains in mind. This isn't compliance for compliance's sake. It's the reason soffyn exists.

1. Our commitment

soffyn is committed to meeting or exceeding WCAG 2.1 Level AA accessibility standards across our website and app. This means:

  • Sufficient color contrast for all text and interactive elements
  • Keyboard navigability throughout the site and app
  • Screen reader compatibility (NVDA, JAWS, VoiceOver)
  • No flashing or strobing content that could trigger seizures
  • Clear, plain language — no jargon, no walls of text
  • Logical heading structure for assistive technology
  • Alt text for all meaningful images
  • Focus indicators on all interactive elements

2. Built for neurodivergent minds

soffyn is designed specifically for the cognitive accessibility needs of neurodivergent users, including those with ADHD, autism, dyslexia, anxiety, and sensory processing differences. This includes:

  • Calm, low-stimulation visual design with no autoplay, pop-ups, or unexpected motion
  • Short, scannable copy — nothing that demands sustained focus to understand
  • Flexible interaction — use the app however works for your brain, at your own pace
  • No time pressure, no streak penalties, no notifications designed to induce anxiety
  • Quick-capture tools for when thoughts move faster than typing
  • Focus timer built for ADHD work patterns, not neurotypical productivity expectations

3. Known limitations and ongoing work

We are a pre-launch product built by a small team. While we are deeply committed to accessibility, we acknowledge that no product is perfect and accessibility is ongoing work, not a checklist.

Current known areas of improvement:

  • Full app accessibility audit is scheduled prior to September 2026 launch
  • Captions and transcripts for any future video content will be provided
  • Accessibility testing with neurodivergent users is part of our beta program

4. Feedback

If you encounter any accessibility barriers on soffyn.com or in the soffyn app, please tell us. We take every report seriously and will work to address issues promptly.

Email: hello@soffyn.com

Your feedback directly shapes how we build. Thank you for helping us do better.

5. Third-party content

soffyn.com may include links to third-party services. We cannot guarantee the accessibility of external sites, but we will prioritize partnerships with accessible platforms wherever possible.
